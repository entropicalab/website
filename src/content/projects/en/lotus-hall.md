brief: |
  the program was an event hall serving a cultural district outside shanghai, long hours of dense
  occupancy, big west glazing in the early scheme, and a client target of leed gold without losing
  the daylight that gave the room its character.

  the climate is cfa: hot humid summers, cold dry winters. the hot half does the damage. the brief
  asked for "tropical thinking applied to a subtropical site," and that became the design rule.
approach: |
  three small models, asked early: solar radiation on the west façade by hour, daylight
  autonomy at the seating plane, and cooling load by envelope variant. each result rewrote
  one piece of the section. shading depth was sized to cut peak radiation by 60% while
  preserving 75% of the daylight the room needed at noon.

  the envelope decisions are conservative on paper, high-performance glazing, deep
  external shading, a buffered roof, but stack into a number that pays the cooling bill
  back within the first six years of operation.
strategies:
