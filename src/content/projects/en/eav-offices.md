brief: |
  the eav offices project in pueblo nuevo proposed the modernisation of a three-storey,
  1,290 m² office building. the client requested a redesign of both façade and interior to
  achieve a contemporary corporate image while improving environmental performance. the
  goal was a workplace that balanced openness with the necessary hierarchy of organisation.
approach: |
  the design strategy focused on the façade as the building's most visible and impactful
  element. we introduced a system of expanded-mesh screens, horizontal louvres, and glass
  shading that worked together to control daylight, reduce heat gain, and improve user
  comfort. these passive measures were optimised to enhance energy performance while
  giving the building a renewed architectural identity.

  inside, the project emphasised openness through flexible open-space planning complemented
  by clear spatial hierarchy. concrete and metal were selected for aesthetic restraint and
  ease of long-term maintenance.

  the project was discontinued due to administrative issues, but it remains an example of
  how façade strategies and spatial planning can align to create energy-conscious workplaces.
